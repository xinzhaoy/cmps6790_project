Introduction:

Interest in electric vehicles (EVs) is growing. To gain a comprehensive understanding of the electric vehicle landscape, I aimed to explore three key data sets using Washington state as an example: electric vehicle ownership data, population and housing statistics, and alternative fuel stations.

Datasets

The following two datasets have all the necessary information I need in order to build prediction model and quantify discrimination in credit card application. I also provided descriptions of two datasets as well as how I am going to use them.

Dataset 1: Electric Vehicle Population Data

https://data.wa.gov/Transportation/Electric-Vehicle-Population-Data/f6w7-q2d2

This dataset shows the Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) that are currently registered through Washington State Department of Licensing (DOL).

Question: How have electric vehicle registrations evolved over the years, and are there discernible regional trends or hotspots?

Dataset 2: Alternative Fueling Stations from U.S. Department of Transportation

https://data-usdot.opendata.arcgis.com/datasets/usdot::alternative-fueling-stations/about

Question: whcih county has the most electric charging station

Dataset 3: 2020 Census data

https://ofm.wa.gov/washington-data-research/population-demographics/decennial-census/2020-census-everyone-counts/2020-census-what-you-need-know/2020-census-data-releases

The apportionment population represents the first official 2020 Census numbers released by the U.S. Census Bureau.

Question: Is there a correlation between population density and the adoption of electric vehicles, and are areas with higher population density experiencing more substantial EV adoption?
